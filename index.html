I'm Fenil Bardoliya, an MS graduate in Computer Science from Arizona State University. I work at the Complex Data Reasoning and Analysis Lab (CoRAL), advised by Dr. Vivek Gupta. Previously I was a Computer Vision Research Aide at DREAMS LAB and interned at Samsung Semiconductor India Research. I received my B.E. in Computer Science from BITS Pilani.

My research focuses on natural language processing, text-to-table generation, and multimodal reasoning—building systems that turn unstructured text into interpretable, structured data and that reason robustly across modalities.

Research

Broadly, I work at the intersection of:

  • Text-to-Table & Information Extraction — Schema-guided extraction, proposition-based reasoning, and table generation from long-form text
  • Multimodal LLMs — Evaluation under distribution shift, robustness, and grounding (e.g. Perceptual Observatory, CVPR Workshop)
  • NL2SQL & Interactive Systems — Real-time question answering and visualization over dynamic data (e.g. SportSQL)
I aim to build practical, interpretable systems that improve how we extract and reason over structured information from text and multimodal data.
